Reflecting on Twelve Pillars

The book has been profoundly impactful, prompting deep reflection and motivating me to pursue self-improvement actively. The insights from each chapter have offered valuable lessons that continue to shape my personal growth journey.

This collection of insights offers profound lessons that I can apply to various aspects of my life. Below is a meaningful reflection on how I can integrate these lessons into my personal growth and development:

  • Make Time for the Things and People You Love:
    Days are precious, and the way we choose to spend our time shapes our lives. By prioritizing meaningful relationships and activities, I can ensure that my time is used wisely and invested in what truly matters to me.

  • The Thief of Joy is Comparison:
    Comparing myself to others detracts from my happiness. Instead, I should focus on surrounding myself with people who inspire me to grow, allowing their positive influence to push me forward without feeling diminished by their success.

  • Set Goals and Take Action:
    Life is about setting clear goals and taking action without delay. By focusing on the present and not dwelling on past mistakes, I can build a future free from regret, as it’s always better to start now than never.

  • Dream Big and Embrace Change:
    Many people never allow themselves to dream, and even fewer pursue those dreams. To chase my ambitions, I must be willing to leave behind relationships or situations that no longer serve me, understanding that growth often requires difficult decisions.

  • Manage Relationships Wisely:
    My friends and associates have a significant impact on my thoughts and behavior. By categorizing relationships into disassociation, limited association, and expanded association, I can ensure I spend my time with those who positively contribute to my growth and limit negative influences.

  • The Weight of Regret is Heavier than Discipline:
    While discipline requires effort, it is far lighter than the burden of regret. Staying committed to my goals now will save me from the pain of missed opportunities in the future.

  • Strive to Become What You Seek to Attract:
    To attract the kind of people and opportunities I want in life, I must first work on embodying those qualities. By continuously improving myself, I align with the things and people I aspire to be surrounded by.

  • Never Stop Learning:
    Learning is a lifelong pursuit. I must remain open to new knowledge, never assuming I know it all, and consistently seeking opportunities to expand my understanding through reading, experiences, and conversations.

In reflecting on these lessons, it’s clear that they collectively advocate for intentional living, making deliberate choices about how I spend my time, who I surround myself with, and how I pursue my goals. I can maximize personal fulfilment, growth, and success by applying these principles in my life.

A Deep Dive into My Degree

I am currently pursuing a bachelor's degree in Computer Science. Throughout my studies, I have developed three key skills:

  1. Time management: The ability to prioritize tasks and manage my time efficiently has been crucial in balancing coursework and personal commitments.

  2. The Strength of Collaboration: Working with peers has highlighted the power of teamwork, allowing us to combine our strengths and enhance our problem-solving capabilities.

  3. The Value of Emotional Intelligence: Understanding and managing emotions—both my own and those of others—has proven essential in fostering strong relationships and navigating challenges effectively.
